Built Around Real Track Weekends

A Better Way to Keep Track of the Track

A race weekend moves quickly. Pressures change, setup decisions accumulate, sessions run together, and important details can end up scattered across paper notes, screenshots, text messages, and memory.

MotoTrack is being built by racers who understand that challenge. It is designed to organize what happened before and after each session in a clearer, more consistent record.

Enter information that stays consistent once. Record what changes between sessions. Debrief while the details are still fresh.

Before Each Session

Record the plan before heading onto the track.

  • Planned setup changes

03

After Each Session

Capture what happened while it is still fresh.

  • Post-session hot pressures and tire temperatures
  • Lap information and rider feel
  • Tire wear and setup-change results
  • Fork-travel observations

MotoTrack is being designed to organize information such as these race-weekend details. Beta features may change during testing.

A Race-Weekend Record, Not a Replacement for Your Team

What MotoTrack Is Intended to Be

  • A structured race-weekend notebook
  • A rider-controlled session history
  • A way to connect pressures, tires, setup changes, lap information, and rider feedback
  • A product shaped by racer feedback

What MotoTrack Is Not

  • Official race timing or scoring
  • Race control
  • A replacement for a coach, mechanic, crew chief, suspension specialist, or tire vendor
  • Automatic suspension tuning or ideal-pressure selection
  • A crash-risk prediction or safety-critical system
  • A public rider-tracking platform

Private By Default

Your Race Data Stays Private by Default

Session notes, setup information, pressures, tire observations, lap data, race number, and rider feedback remain private by default.

MotoTrack does not automatically share information with other riders, coaches, teams, vendors, tire manufacturers, or the public. Any future sharing will require the rider to choose what is shared and who receives access.

  • No automatic public profile
  • No automatic coach, vendor, or manufacturer access
  • No public GPS trace
  • No sale of individual rider session data

Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can participate?

The beta is intended for eligible PanAmSBK amateur racers. Access is limited and each request is reviewed before an invitation is issued.

Is the beta free?

Approved participants receive free access through the end of the 2026 PanAmSBK season. No payment information is required during the beta.

Will I be charged automatically after the season?

No. Any future paid option will be explained before a purchase or subscription is required.

Does MotoTrack provide setup recommendations?

The beta focuses on recording and organizing rider-confirmed information. It does not replace a coach, mechanic, crew chief, suspension specialist, or tire vendor.

Is MotoTrack official race timing?

No. Official results, grids, scoring, and timing remain with PanAmSBK and its designated timing providers.

Is my information public?

No. Race-weekend and session information is private by default. Nothing is automatically published or shared.

Can I share my beta access link?

No. Private access links are intended only for the approved participant and should not be posted publicly or forwarded.
